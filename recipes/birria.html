Birria

Prep: 30 minutes

Cook: 3 hours 40 minutes

Additional: 6 hours

Total: 10 hours 10 minutes

Servings: 10

Yield: 10 servings

Ingredients

  • 3 (8 ounce) beef short ribs
  • 1 (2 1/2 pound) beef chuck
  • 4 teaspoons of kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons of dried Mexican oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cloves
  • 7 peppers dried guajillo chilies
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 large onion, roughly chopped
  • 6 cloves of garlic, peeled
  • 1 (1 inch) piece of fresh ginger, thinly sliced
  • 3 large tomatoes, cored and chopped
  • 2 cups of water
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 1/4 cup of white vinegar
  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons of finely diced white onion
  • 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh cilantro

Directions

  1. Step 1

    2. Cut each beef short rib through the middle, all the way down to the bone.

    Cut beef chuck in half; cut each half into 3- to 4-inch pieces.

  2. Step 2

    3. Place beef chuck and short ribs into a soup pot and season with salt,black pepper,

    oregano, cumin, cinnamon, and cloves. Toss very thoroughly until the meat

    is evenly coated with the spices,2 to 3 mintues.

  3. Step 3

    4. Cover and transfer into the refrigerator for atleast 6 hours, or overnight.

  4. Step 4

    5. Prep guajillo chiles by snipping off stems with scissors; slice open and scrape or

    shake out the seeds onto a plate to discard.

  5. Step 5

    6. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add dried guajillo chiles and toss in

    the hot oil, about 30 seconds. Add chopped onion, garlic, and ginger; toss to

    combine. Add tomatoes and water, raise heat to high, and bring the mixture to a

    simmer. Reduce heat to medium-low and let simmer for 30 minutes.

  6. Step 6

    7. Turn off heat and use an immersion blender to blend thi mixture as smooth as

    possible, or transfer to a regular blender, working in batches as needed

  7. Step 7

    8. Remove the soup pot with meat from the refrigerator. Strain the reserved chili

    mixture into the pot using a large mesh strainer. Add bay leaves, honey, vinegar, and

    chicken broth.

  8. Step 8

    9. Bring to a boil over high heat, and then lower to medium-low. Simmer gently, stirring

    occasionally, until the meat is falling-apart tender, 3 to 4 hours.

    Sjun fat off of the top and reserve.

  9. Step 9

    10. Place some chunks of beef into a soup bowl and ladle some of the cooking liquid

    over top. Top with white onion and chopped cilantro and squeeze lime juice over top.
